About Zhong Kui

Zhong Kui (钟馗) is a legendary figure in Chinese folklore, often recognized as the "Demon Queller." He is believed to have the power to capture and command ghosts and evil spirits, protecting people from harm. Stories describe him as a brilliant scholar who, despite his talents, was denied official recognition due to his appearance. After his tragic death, he was granted a divine role as protector against evil.

Cultural Significance

In Chinese tradition, Zhong Kui is regarded as a symbol of justice and protection. His fierce image is often placed on household doors during festivals such as the Lunar New Year and Dragon Boat Festival to ward off evil spirits and bring peace to the family. He is also depicted in opera, paintings, and temple murals as a mighty guardian with a sword, commanding an army of spirits.

Legends

Many legends surround Zhong Kui. The Scholar’s Tragedy: Zhong Kui was said to be a top scholar who failed the imperial examination due to his appearance, leading to his despair and death. The Emperor’s Dream: In another tale, Emperor Xuanzong of Tang dreamed of Zhong Kui slaying demons, after which he ordered his image to be painted for protection. These stories established Zhong Kui as a revered figure in both folklore and imperial culture.
